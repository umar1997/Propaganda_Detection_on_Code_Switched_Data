Propaganda Detection and Annotation

This website allows you to both Detect Propaganda as well as Annotate Text Examples according to the Propaganda Techniques.

In this thesis we discuss propaganda as a form of communication aimed at influencing the opinions and behavior of a target audience towards a particular agenda. With the rise of the internet and social media platforms, the spread of propaganda has become widespread and can have a significant impact on individuals' attitudes and behavior. Most propaganda detection work has been done on high-resourced languages, such as English, leaving low-resourced languages, such as Roman Urdu, largely unexplored. Code-switching, which involves mixing multiple languages in the same text, is common in low-resourced language communities and can make propaganda detection more challenging. We propose a novel NLP task of detecting propaganda techniques in code-switched data involving English and Roman Urdu. To support this task, we create a corpus of 1030 code-switched texts and manually annotate them with 20 propaganda techniques. Additionally, we developed a web-based annotation platform to facilitate the annotation process. This platform has a user interface to detect propaganda on text and allows for annotation of propaganda techniques on specific spans of text. We run experiments using several pre-trained multilingual and cross-lingual language models and evaluated their performances. We find that XLM RoBERTa fine-tuned on Roman Urdu outperforms all other baseline NLP models on our task and dataset. Our work represents a significant contribution to the field of NLP and the study of propaganda detection.
